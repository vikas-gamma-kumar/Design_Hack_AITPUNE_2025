About Us

Vision and Mission

Vision


  • To become a "Globally Recognised" technical institute providing world class education and research facilities to the wards of Defence personnel.

Mission


  • Provide the right environment, to the wards of Defence personnel, for development of physical, intellectual, emotional and spiritual quotients, with a view to produce total quality engineers.

  • Create an ecosystem which can foster the culture of research, innovation, creative thinking and higher studies.

  • Develop an education system which creates entrepreneurs and technology leaders who are committed towards sustainable development of society and nation building.

Director's Corner



Brig Abhay A Bhat (Retd)

M Tech, MPM, B Tech, FIETE

Director, AIT

Profile

Brig Abhay A Bhat, an alumnus of National Defence Academy (NDA) was commissioned into the Corps of Signals in December 1982. He has done his BTech in Electronics and telecommunications from Military College of Telecommunications Engineering, Mhow (MCTE). Brig Bhat has graduated from the prestigious Defence Services Staff College, Wellington, where army officers are trained in the nuances of staff function of various operations. Due to his professional calibre, he was sponsored by the Army to attain M Tech in Computer Science and Engineering from IIT, Kanpur. The officer has also acquired a Master’s degree in Personnel Management from Pune University.


He has served in counter insurgency areas of Assam and J&K. Brig Bhat commanded an Armoured Division Signal Regiment during op PARAKRAM, and has served on the staff of MS Branch, Integrated HQ of MoD which is responsible for career management of officers. He was also involved in implementing IT projects for developing Information Technology Infrastructure in remote areas of North East.


Brig Bhat has been awarded the Chief of Army Staff Commendation Card in 2011, for outstanding work in the field of Signal Intelligence in support of the operations in J & K.


The officer was the Commander of the Faculty of Communications Engineering at MCTE from 2011 to 2014; where he was responsible for conduct of the engineering degree, PG and Diploma courses for Army officers. He pioneered many automation projects and modernisation of laboratories, as well as developed the eLearning environment in MCTE. He was the Chairman of the IETE (Institute of Electronics and Telecom Engineers) Sub centre, Mhow from 2011 to 2014.


Brig Bhat has written articles in defence publications on eLearning and Knowledge management. He has participated in many Seminars and Conferences as panel member in the fields of Next Generation Networks, Cyber Security and Defence Communication Technologies.


Before his retirement, Brig Abhay Bhat was posted as the Chief Signal Officer of the Maharashtra, Gujarat and Goa Area; responsible for the Army telecommunication and IT networks in these states.


Brig Bhat took premature retirement from Indian Army on 11 Apr 2017, and joined Army Institute of Technology as it’s 9th Director on 15 Apr 2017. In last six years he has substantially improved the academic, technical and administrative infrastructure and reputation of the college.


Under his leadership the college is enhancing it’s footprint in innovation, entrepreneurship and research. AIT is also poised for a major expansion drive based on his vision and roadmap.


Director's Message


Army Institute of Technology is a Premier Engineering College in the region. Having received the “Best Professional College (Urban)” Trophy in Pune University in Feb 2017, it has been rated amongst top three colleges in Pune region by all the ranking agencies.


AIT has had a large number of achievements. Most of them attributed to the wonderful students, full of energy and competitive spirit. One of the most notable achievers was Capt Shipra Muzumdar of 2001 batch, who climbed Mt Everest on 03 Jul 2005 as a member of Indian Army first all Women expedition.


AIT students are always in the news for their technical achievements, and innovations. M Rajesh, 2002 batch Computer Engineer was felicitated by Bill Gates for winning the Microsoft sponsored Imagica Cup. Lavpreet Sharma, 2014 batch Mechanical Engineer, made AIT proud by winning the Sword of Honour in the Airforce Academy.


AIT students have been winning many hackathons and innovation competitions at National and International level. AIT students have won the prestigious KPIT, Sparkle innovation championship, two years in succession in 2019 and 2020. Both innovations were in the field of out of box devices to generate clean renewable energy. In 2020 AIT teams won top three prizes in NEC Hackathon. Sekhar Kumar Singh won part of winning Multi-National team of UNESCO India- Africa Hackathon. AIT team also came runner up in Sainya Ranakshetram 2.0 – Capture the Flag in Dec 2022. AIT created history when its three teams swept the top three prizes in NEC hackathon at IIT Delhi, which had a theme of Use of Technology in education for students in rural areas. Though predominantly UG college, AIT students have been at the forefront of entrepreneurship and startups. AIT has over 50 alumni who are running successful startups.


AIT students have enjoyed excellent placements over the years. The 2022-23 batch has achieved 96% placements with an average salary of Rs. 14.2 lakh. A good number of AIT students of second and third year have enrolled for internships with reputed companies such as Microsoft, Google, Amazon, Deutsche Bank, Dream Plug and some Indian start-ups. Many of them are drawing monthly stipend of over Rs. One lakh. Internships with such reputed organisations provide a great launch pad for amazing careers. One such student Mr Abhishek Kumar of TE(IT) has been selected for an internship with Amazon London, at a monthly stipend of equivalent to Rs. Four lakh approx, another Shristy Thakur SE (E&TC) student has proceeded for an internship in Jun 23 at Barclays Bank in UK for monthly stipend of equivalent to Rs. 3 lakhs.


With the approval of the AIT Growth plan by HQ AWES, the intake of AIT is set to double in next 3-4 years. We are already increasing the intake in IT stream by 60 in AY 2023-24. We will be introducing a UG course in BE (Robotics and Automation). AIT is also looking at introducing new PG programs, establishing Centres of Excellence and Research Centres. The ME course in Data Science has already been launched this year. Apart from regular degree courses, AIT is also starting short term diploma/certification courses in collaboration with reputed training institutes in niche technology fields such as Embedded Systems, VLSI, Data Science etc, to meet the fast growing demands of industry.


All these student achievements cannot be accomplished without commitment, guidance and support of the committed faculty of AIT. The faculty too is going all out to enhance their knowledge, reputation and improve the research footprint of the college.


AIT prides itself in having an excellent academic extracurricular and sports infrastructure. This ensures all round development of students and total quality engineers passing out from AIT.

‘Onward to glory’, ‘Jai Hind’
